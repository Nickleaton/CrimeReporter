Tuesday 3 February, 2026
A convicted drug dealer has been ordered to pay back nearly £400k or face an additional jail term.
A Proceeds of Crime Act (POCA) hearing was held at Leeds Crown Court on Monday (2 February) where
Curtis Clark, 25, was ordered to pay back £393,363.77 within three months of face a further four
years in prison.
Curtis was sentenced to 65 months imprisonment in August last year after pleading guilty to being
concerned in the supply of Class A and B drugs and money laundering. He was linked to an organised
crime group who had control of a drug dealing line responsible for the sale of large quantities of
drugs throughout Leeds and West Yorkshire.
His arrest followed search warrants being executed in November 2024 at his home address in the
Whitkirk area of Leeds and other premises he had control over. During these searches in excess of
£270k in cash, two motor vehicles, a large amount of designer clothing and three Rolex watches
valued at £100k were all recovered.
Detective Inspector Alastair Nicholls said: “Organised criminality is a blight on our communities,
with individuals like Curtis preying on the vulnerability of others for their own gain.
“When Curtis was sentenced, the court heard how he ‘loved the lifestyle’ with his Rolexes and fast
cars. This POCA hearing has ensured that on his release from prison, he will not be returning to
that lifestyle. Instead, his assets will be sold and the money recovered will go towards projects to
disrupt criminality and build communities.
“I hope this action sends the message that if you are profiting from crime then we will not only
look to arrest and charge you, but we will also use all available powers to strip you of any wealth
you have gained from your illegal activities.”
