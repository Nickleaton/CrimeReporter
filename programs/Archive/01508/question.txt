Title

Man jailed for Sheffield shootings following quick work by Armed Crime Team
===============

Source
===============
SYP
===============

text
===============
Quick work by officers responding to reports of two linked firearms discharges has resulted in a violent man being put behind bars.On 3 April, we received reports of a gun being fired at a property on Daniel Hill Street in Sheffield. A short time later, a follow-up report of a motorcyclist being knocked off his bike and shot at on Rivelin Valley Road was also made.Armed officers attended and found empty firearm cartridges at both addresses.Swift work by officers, including gathering witness statements and undergoing CCTV trawls, identified vehicles linked to both incidents and a named suspect - Marcus Ned.Ned was known to the victim and had launched a targeted attack intent on causing the victim harm.Armed response vehicles were deployed to an address linked to Ned to arrest the dangerous individual. However, as the net was closing around the 37-year-old, he made the decision to flee in a naïve attempt to avoid arrest.Ned scaled down a two-story block of flats before leading officers on a foot chase. Thinking he had successfully evaded officers, Ned hid in a garden of a nearby property.Unfortunately for Ned, colleagues from the National Police Air Service (NPAS) were on hand and had tracked his escape attempt - leading to him being promptly located and detained.A search of the property Ned was fleeing uncovered a firearm and ammunition which matched the cartridges found at the scene of both discharges.Ned, of Fairburn Place, Sheffield, was charged with two counts of possession of a firearm with intent to cause fear of violence and one charge of attempt to cause grievous bodily harm - all of which he pleaded guilty to.Yesterday (Tuesday 3 February) at Sheffield Crown Court, Ned was sentenced to nine years and nine months in prison.DC Hughes, of our Armed Crime Team, said:"Firearms have the potential to inflict serious harm on our communities. Thankfully no one was injured by Ned's reckless criminality but this isn't always the case."We have seen firsthand in South Yorkshire the damage firearms can cause, and this sentencing shows that anyone intent on becoming involved in firearm-related criminality will be found and dealt with by the courts."We continue to urge anyone with information on the use of firearms in South Yorkshire to get in touch with us. You - our local communities - are vital in helping us tackling gun crime. If you know something, tell us so we can act on it."

Quick work by officers responding to reports of two linked firearms discharges has resulted in a violent man being put behind bars.
On 3 April, we received reports of a gun being fired at a property on Daniel Hill Street in Sheffield. A short time later, a follow-up report of a motorcyclist being knocked off his bike and shot at on Rivelin Valley Road was also made.
Armed officers attended and found empty firearm cartridges at both addresses.
Swift work by officers, including gathering witness statements and undergoing CCTV trawls, identified vehicles linked to both incidents and a named suspect - Marcus Ned.
Ned was known to the victim and had launched a targeted attack intent on causing the victim harm.
Armed response vehicles were deployed to an address linked to Ned to arrest the dangerous individual. However, as the net was closing around the 37-year-old, he made the decision to flee in a naïve attempt to avoid arrest.
Ned scaled down a two-story block of flats before leading officers on a foot chase. Thinking he had successfully evaded officers, Ned hid in a garden of a nearby property.
Unfortunately for Ned, colleagues from the National Police Air Service (NPAS) were on hand and had tracked his escape attempt - leading to him being promptly located and detained.
A search of the property Ned was fleeing uncovered a firearm and ammunition which matched the cartridges found at the scene of both discharges.
Ned, of Fairburn Place, Sheffield, was charged with two counts of possession of a firearm with intent to cause fear of violence and one charge of attempt to cause grievous bodily harm - all of which he pleaded guilty to.
Yesterday (Tuesday 3 February) at Sheffield Crown Court, Ned was sentenced to nine years and nine months in prison.
DC Hughes, of our Armed Crime Team, said:
"Firearms have the potential to inflict serious harm on our communities. Thankfully no one was injured by Ned's reckless criminality but this isn't always the case.
"We have seen firsthand in South Yorkshire the damage firearms can cause, and this sentencing shows that anyone intent on becoming involved in firearm-related criminality will be found and dealt with by the courts.
"We continue to urge anyone with information on the use of firearms in South Yorkshire to get in touch with us. You - our local communities - are vital in helping us tackling gun crime. If you know something, tell us so we can act on it."
