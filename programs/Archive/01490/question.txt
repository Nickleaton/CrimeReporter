Misconduct investigation sees corrupt former prison officer jailed
===============

text
===============
A former Doncaster prison officer has been jailed after engaging in a romantic relationship with an inmate.
Charlotte Winstanley, 27, of Coronation Road, Doncaster was working as a prison officer at HMP Lindholme when she entered into a romantic relationship with inmate Jabhari Blair, who was serving a 12 year and six month sentence for wounding and firearms offences.
After reports of their relationship came to light the following month, our officers were called in by prison officials and began an investigation into Winstanley. Through extensive enquiries, we uncovered the extent of her relationship with Blair.
A review of the prison’s CCTV revealed evidence of inappropriate contact between the pair, and a search of Blair’s cell uncovered cannabis, a mobile phone, and a USB stick.
It was discovered that Winstanley had conveyed the mobile phone to Blair. The phone was analysed and found to have been a device previously registered to Winstanley.
Analysis of Winstanley’s mobile phone and email records revealed intimate messages, images and videos sent between herself and Blair, as well as discussion of how she brought the mobile phone into the prison.
Following Winstanley’s suspension and arrest in November 2022, her car and home were searched. From her bedroom, officers recovered letters between the pair and photos of Blair.
Both defendants responded with “no comment” to questions in their police interviews.
Winstanley was charged with being a holder of a public office and wilfully misconducting herself, and without authority transmitting an image from inside a prison.
Jabhari Blair, 30, of no fixed abode was charged with possession of a Class B drug (namely cannabis), without authority possessing a mobile phone in prison, and without authority possessing a USB device in prison. Blair pleaded guilty to the offences at a previous hearing on 30 January.
Winstanley was sentenced on Monday (2 February) at Sheffield Crown Court. She was jailed for two years and six months.
Blair was sentenced at the same court on Monday and received a 13-month prison sentence consecutive to his 12-year sentence.
DC Scott Jarvis, the officer in charge of the case, said:“Winstanley’s actions will no doubt be shocking to members of the public who rightly expect the highest standards from those who work within His Majesty’s Prisons and Probation Service”
“The prison acted immediately when evidence of her misconduct came to light and they fully supported our extensive enquiries which have led to Monday’s outcome”
“Through a thorough investigation we were able to chart the course of Winstanley’s relationship with Blair and provide irrefutable evidence of her criminal misconduct as well as his crimes”
“I hope this result shows that no-one is above the law and we will work without fear or favour to bring all offenders to justice.”
Blair and Winstanley pleaded not guilty to conspiring to convey a List B prohibited item (namely a mobile phone) into a prison. The charge will remain on file.
